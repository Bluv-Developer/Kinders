The Good Deed Challenge

Students are actively encouraged to perform at least one intentional act of kindness daily. These can include:

  • Holding doors open for others
  • Giving genuine compliments to classmates
  • Helping a classmate with schoolwork
  • Sitting with someone who is alone at lunch
  • Standing up for someone being treated unfairly

This program creates a ripple effect of kindness throughout the school, making compassion a daily habit.

Informal Community Builders

The Kinders team plans and hosts informal, fun activities outside of the standard school curriculum to break down social barriers and build stronger relationships between different groups of students.

Activities include:

  • Lunchtime games and social mixers
  • After-school hobby clubs
  • Peer mentorship programs
  • Collaborative art projects promoting unity
  • Community service projects

Disarming the Bully: The Reaction Principle

A core advocacy effort that focuses on empowering victims and bystanders with strategies to neutralize bullying behavior by teaching them how not to react, thereby removing the bully's source of power.

Key components include:

  • Teaching non-reactive responses to bullying
  • Empowering bystanders to intervene safely
  • Building confidence in potential targets
  • Creating peer-enforced safe spaces
  • Workshops on conflict de-escalation techniques

Our Organization

Understanding the student-led structure and independence of the Kinders Operation

Student-Led and Managed

Kinders was created and is solely managed by one student, Elijah Shepherd (the "Operation Lead"), with the help of a Kinders Staff and Kinders Helpers (collectively referred to as "Kindes").

Important Disclaimer

Kinders is explicitly NOT created, managed, or formally affiliated with Darby Middle School. All activities are carried out by the students in a private, non-official capacity.

The initiative operates under the belief that administrative actions alone are insufficient and that true, lasting change requires empowering students to lead with empathy and take ownership of their school's social environment.

Our organization is built on the foundation of peer leadership, with students taking responsibility for creating the positive change they wish to see in their school community.
